What is calisthenics?

The word «calisthenics» is made of 2 greecs words, Kalos which means beauty, it shows the artistic side of the discipline and Sthenos which means strength, for the mental and physique strength and the determination. It can be translated by "The beauty of strength".

This sport is made of different branches that come from America and the East. These branches are in development because they are really recent.

The earliest traces of this sport can be found in early 19th century in Sweden. Calisthenics can be seen combining classical flexibility and agility exercises with more strength ones. The first nomination could come from an american school teacher, Catharine Esther Beecher. She has written a book entitled "Physiology And Calisthenics: For Schools And Families" which aims to understand the functions of the human body and shows the importance of physical exercise. In fact, she has introduced into her classes a form of gymnastics which, like calisthenics, works on flexibility but also develops bodyweight strength. Her work and her approach to sport are also aimed at achieving a healthy lifestyle.

After that, until the 21st century, there didn't seem to be anything new in the discipline. But in 2008, the American Hannibal Lanham, aka Hannibal For Kings, posted a video of one of his workouts, which garnered millions of views on youtube. After that, Street Workout was born. This is another common name for callisthenics, but it actually refers to practising one of the branches of callisthenics, which will be presented in detail later, but on the street.

My practice

I have trained Set and Reps way before I discovered the calisthenics. I got motivated by a friend in the 6th grad and started doing push-ups and some basic bodyweight exercises such as gainage. That said, I wasn't at all consistent about my workouts and didn't really know what I was doing. This period lasted until around 2021, when, after coronavirus, I discovered what was possible by training using only my own body weight. That's when I started working on the fundamentals, mainly L-sit and pull-ups.
I progressed fairly quickly, doing a lot of pull-ups at home on a Roman chair and in parks with friends. Unfortunately, I had less active training periods... But since the start of the 2022 school year, I've been very active. I got my first muscle-ups in February with pretty poor form and recently managed to do some in "Slow" which means in slow motion so without momentum. At the same time I'm working on the Front Lever, but it's a very complicated skill to start with. For a while now I've been working on a single-leg form and I can almost hold the full version for two seconds.

My goals

My goals now are to get the Front Lever, improve my strength in pull-ups and push (push-ups and semi-planche) and then catch up in handstand. I've still got a long way to go to my final goal, which is to do Pelican Pushups. This exercise is... extremely difficult. Mainly performed on rings, it consists in doing a sort of pump but starting from a Full Plank and descending to a supinated Back Lever and then rising again.
